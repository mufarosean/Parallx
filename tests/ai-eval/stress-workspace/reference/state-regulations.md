# State Insurance Regulations — Reference Guide

**Jurisdiction:** Example State
**Last Updated:** December 2023
**Compiled by:** Compliance Department

---

## Chapter 1: Auto Insurance Requirements

### Section 1.1 — Mandatory Minimum Coverage

All registered vehicles in the state must carry the following minimum liability coverage:

- Bodily Injury: $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident
- Property Damage: $25,000 per accident
- Uninsured Motorist: $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident (can be rejected in writing)

Failure to maintain minimum coverage results in:
- First offense: $500 fine + license suspension until proof of insurance
- Second offense: $1,000 fine + 90-day license suspension
- Third offense: $2,500 fine + 1-year suspension + possible vehicle impoundment

### Section 1.2 — No-Fault Provisions

This state does NOT operate under a no-fault system. Liability is determined by fault, and the at-fault party's insurer pays for damages. Comparative negligence rules apply (see Section 1.4).

### Section 1.3 — Total Loss Threshold

A vehicle is a total loss when the cost of repair exceeds **75% of the vehicle's actual cash value**. The insurer must:

1. Provide a written total loss valuation within 10 business days of inspection
2. Include comparable vehicle listings to support the ACV determination
3. Allow the insured to obtain an independent appraisal at their own expense
4. Accept the independent appraisal if the difference exceeds 10% of their valuation

### Section 1.4 — Comparative Negligence

This state follows **modified comparative negligence (51% bar)**:

- You can recover damages if you are 50% or less at fault
- Your recovery is reduced by your percentage of fault
- If you are 51% or more at fault, you recover nothing
- Joint and several liability applies to economic damages
- Several liability only for non-economic damages

**Example:** You are 30% at fault. Total damages = $100,000.
- Your recovery: $100,000 × (1 − 0.30) = $70,000

### Section 1.5 — Statute of Limitations

| Claim Type | Limitation Period |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Bodily Injury | 2 years from date of injury |
| Property Damage | 3 years from date of damage |
| Uninsured Motorist | 2 years from date of injury |
| Bad Faith | 3 years from denial of claim |

### Section 1.6 — Anti-Stacking

This state prohibits stacking of uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage across multiple vehicles on the same policy. The UM/UIM limit applies per accident, not per vehicle.

## Chapter 2: Homeowners Insurance Regulations

### Section 2.1 — Required Disclosures

All homeowners policies must include the following disclosures:

1. **Flood exclusion notice** — clear statement that flooding is not covered and separate NFIP coverage is available
2. **Replacement cost vs ACV election** — insured must affirmatively choose
3. **Ordinance or law coverage** — notice that rebuilding to current code may not be fully covered unless this endorsement is purchased
4. **Mold limitation** — notice of any sub-limit on mold remediation

### Section 2.2 — Claims Handling Requirements

Insurers must:
- Acknowledge receipt of a claim within 15 calendar days
- Begin investigation within 15 calendar days of acknowledgment
- Accept or deny the claim within 30 calendar days of receiving complete documentation
- Pay accepted claims within 10 business days of acceptance

Failure to meet these timelines constitutes unfair claims practice under state law.

### Section 2.3 — Cancellation Restrictions

Homeowners policies:
- Cannot be cancelled mid-term except for non-payment, fraud, or material misrepresentation
- Non-renewal notice must be provided at least 60 days before expiration
- After 3+ years of continuous coverage, insurer must provide 90 days notice of non-renewal

### Section 2.4 — Wind/Hail Deductible

The state permits separate percentage deductibles for wind and hail damage:
- Maximum allowable: 5% of Coverage A (dwelling) limit
- Must be clearly disclosed at policy purchase and renewal
- Insured may elect a flat dollar deductible instead (at higher premium)

## Chapter 3: Umbrella/Excess Liability

### Section 3.1 — Underlying Requirements

Umbrella insurers may require minimum underlying coverage limits. These requirements are not state-regulated but are contractual between the umbrella carrier and the insured.

Common minimum requirements:
- Auto: $250,000/$500,000 BI, $100,000 PD
- Homeowners: $300,000 liability
- Watercraft: $300,000 liability (if applicable)

### Section 3.2 — Self-Insured Retention

The SIR on umbrella policies is not regulated, but industry standard is $10,000. Some carriers offer $0 SIR at higher premiums.

## Chapter 4: Claims Practices Act

### Section 4.1 — Prohibited Practices

The following are prohibited unfair claims practices:

1. Misrepresenting pertinent facts or policy provisions
2. Failing to acknowledge and act promptly upon communications
3. Failing to adopt and implement reasonable standards for investigation
4. Refusing to pay claims without conducting a reasonable investigation
5. Not attempting in good faith to effectuate prompt, fair, and equitable settlements when liability is reasonably clear
6. Compelling insureds to institute litigation to recover amounts due by offering substantially less than amounts ultimately recovered
7. Attempting to settle for less than the amount a reasonable person would believe is owed
8. Settling claims for less than the amount the insured is entitled to when the insurer has information in its file that the amount is owed
9. Delaying investigation or payment of claims by requiring duplicate submissions of documentation
10. Making claim payments not accompanied by a statement identifying the coverage for which payment is made

### Section 4.2 — Bad Faith

An insurer acts in bad faith when it:
- Unreasonably denies or delays a claim
- Fails to properly investigate
- Lowballs a settlement when liability and damages are clear
- Violates any provision of the Claims Practices Act

Remedies for bad faith:
- Actual damages (the amount owed under the policy)
- Consequential damages (additional losses caused by the delay/denial)
- Emotional distress damages (in egregious cases)
- Punitive damages (if insurer acted with conscious disregard)
- Attorney fees and costs

### Section 4.3 — Appraisal Process

Either party may invoke the appraisal process when there is a dispute over the amount of loss (not coverage):

1. Each party selects an appraiser within 20 days
2. The two appraisers select an umpire
3. If they cannot agree on an umpire, either party may petition the court
4. Agreement of any two (two appraisers, or one appraiser + umpire) is binding
5. Each party pays its own appraiser; the umpire cost is split

## Chapter 5: Agent Licensing

### Section 5.1 — Requirements

All insurance agents must:
- Pass the state licensing examination
- Complete 24 hours of continuing education every 2 years
- Maintain errors & omissions (E&O) insurance of at least $1,000,000
- Register with the state Department of Insurance
- Display license number on all business correspondence

### Section 5.2 — Fiduciary Duty

Agents owe a fiduciary duty to their clients to:
- Recommend appropriate coverage based on the client's needs
- Disclose material information about policy limitations
- Process applications and endorsements in a timely manner
- Maintain confidentiality of client information

Breach of fiduciary duty may result in license revocation and civil liability.
