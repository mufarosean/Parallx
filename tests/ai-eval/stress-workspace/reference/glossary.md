# Insurance Glossary

## A

**Actual Cash Value (ACV):** The replacement cost of property minus depreciation. Represents the fair market value at the time of loss.

**Adjuster:** A person who investigates insurance claims and recommends settlement amounts. May be a company employee (staff adjuster) or an independent contractor.

**Agent:** A licensed professional who sells insurance policies on behalf of one or more insurance companies. May be "captive" (representing one company) or "independent" (representing multiple companies).

## B

**Binder:** A temporary agreement providing insurance coverage until the formal policy is issued. Typically valid for 30–60 days.

**Bodily Injury (BI):** Physical harm to a person, including illness, disease, or death resulting from an accident.

## C

**Claim:** A formal request to an insurance company for payment under the terms of a policy.

**Collision Coverage:** Insurance that pays for damage to your vehicle resulting from a collision with another vehicle or object.

**Comparative Negligence:** A legal principle that reduces a plaintiff's recovery by their percentage of fault in causing the accident.

**Comprehensive Coverage:** Insurance that covers damage to your vehicle from non-collision events such as theft, vandalism, weather, and animal strikes.

## D

**Declarations Page (Dec Page):** The front page of an insurance policy listing the named insured, policy number, effective dates, coverage limits, deductibles, and premium.

**Deductible:** The amount the insured pays out of pocket before insurance coverage begins. Higher deductibles generally result in lower premiums.

**Depreciation:** The decrease in value of property due to age, wear and tear, or obsolescence.

**Diminished Value:** The reduction in a vehicle's market value after it has been in an accident and repaired.

## E

**Endorsement:** A written modification to an insurance policy that adds, removes, or changes coverage. Also called a "rider."

**Exclusion:** A specific condition, situation, or circumstance that is not covered by an insurance policy.

## F

**First-Party Claim:** A claim you file with your own insurance company (e.g., collision or comprehensive claim).

**Fraud:** Intentional deception to obtain insurance benefits. A criminal offense in all states.

## G

**Gap Insurance:** Coverage that pays the difference between what you owe on a vehicle loan and the vehicle's ACV if it's totaled.

## I

**Indemnification:** The principle that insurance should restore the insured to the same financial position they were in before the loss — no better, no worse.

## L

**Liability:** Legal responsibility. In insurance, the obligation to pay for damages caused to others.

**Loss Payee:** A party (usually a mortgage company or lender) that has a financial interest in the insured property and receives claim payments.

## N

**Named Insured:** The person(s) specifically listed on the insurance policy declarations page.

**NFIP (National Flood Insurance Program):** A federal program that provides flood insurance through participating insurers.

## P

**Premium:** The amount paid for insurance coverage, typically expressed as an annual, semi-annual, or monthly amount.

**Property Damage (PD):** Damage to someone else's property caused by you or your insured vehicle.

## R

**Replacement Cost:** The cost to replace damaged property with new property of similar kind and quality, without deduction for depreciation.

## S

**Salvage:** Damaged property retained by the insurer after paying a total loss claim. Usually sold at auction.

**Stacking:** Combining coverage limits from multiple vehicles or policies. Prohibited for UM/UIM in some states.

**Subrogation:** The process by which an insurer recovers money from the at-fault party after paying a claim to its insured.

**Surcharge:** An additional premium charge applied after an at-fault accident or traffic violation.

## T

**Third-Party Claim:** A claim filed against another person's insurance company when they are at fault.

**Total Loss:** When the cost of repairing a vehicle exceeds a threshold percentage of its ACV (typically 70–75%).

## U

**Umbrella Policy:** A liability policy that provides additional coverage above the limits of underlying auto and homeowners policies.

**Underinsured Motorist (UIM):** Coverage that pays when the at-fault driver's insurance limits are insufficient to cover your damages.

**Uninsured Motorist (UM):** Coverage that pays when the at-fault driver has no insurance.
