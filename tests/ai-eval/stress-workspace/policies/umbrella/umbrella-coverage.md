## What is Umbrella Insurance?

An umbrella policy provides an extra layer of liability coverage that goes beyond the limits of your underlying auto, homeowners, or watercraft policies. It kicks in when the liability limits on your underlying policies have been exhausted.

## What's Covered

- Bodily injury claims exceeding your auto or homeowners liability limits
- Property damage claims exceeding underlying limits
- Personal injury claims: libel, slander, defamation, false arrest, invasion of privacy
- Landlord liability (up to 4 rental units)
- Legal defense costs (in addition to the umbrella limit, not subtracted from it)

## Underlying Insurance Requirements

To qualify for umbrella coverage, you must maintain minimum limits on your underlying policies:

| Policy Type    | Minimum Required |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Auto — BI      | $250,000/$500,000 |
| Auto — PD      | $100,000 |
| Homeowners     | $300,000 liability |

## Exclusions

1. Business or professional liability
2. Workers' compensation
3. Intentional acts
4. Contractual liability assumed under an agreement
5. Aircraft or watercraft over 50 horsepower (unless scheduled)
6. Communicable disease transmission
7. Sexual molestation or abuse

## How Claims Work

When an underlying policy's liability limit is reached:
1. The underlying insurer pays its full limit
2. You notify the umbrella carrier of the excess claim
3. The umbrella policy pays the remainder up to its limit
4. Defense costs are covered in addition to — not subtracted from — the umbrella limit

## Self-Insured Retention (SIR)

For claims not covered by an underlying policy (e.g., a personal injury claim with no underlying coverage), the insured pays a **$10,000 self-insured retention** before the umbrella responds.
