# Claims Guide — How to File an Auto Insurance Claim

This guide walks you through the complete process of filing a claim with Great Lakes Mutual Insurance Co.

---

## When to File a Claim

File a claim whenever:
- Your vehicle is damaged in an accident (collision or comprehensive)
- You're injured in an accident
- You damage someone else's property or vehicle
- Your vehicle is stolen or vandalized
- An uninsured driver hits you

**Filing Deadline:** You must report the incident within **72 hours**. Failure to report within this window may result in claim denial.

---

## Immediate Steps After an Accident

### At the Scene

1. **Ensure safety first.** Move to a safe location if possible. Turn on hazard lights.
2. **Call 911** if anyone is injured or if the road is blocked.
3. **Call the police.** Request an officer to the scene. A police report is extremely valuable for your claim and is **required** for UM/UIM claims.
4. **Exchange information** with the other driver:
   - Full name
   - Phone number
   - Insurance company and policy number
   - Driver's license number
   - License plate number
   - Vehicle make, model, and color
5. **Document everything with photos:**
   - All vehicle damage (yours and theirs) from multiple angles
   - The overall accident scene (road, intersection, traffic signs)
   - License plates of all vehicles
   - Any visible injuries
   - Road conditions (wet, icy, potholes)
   - Skid marks or debris
6. **Get witness information.** Names and phone numbers of anyone who saw the accident.
7. **Do NOT admit fault** at the scene, even if you think you caused it. Let the insurance companies determine liability.
8. **Write down the police report number** before the officer leaves.

### Within 24 Hours

1. **Call your insurance agent** — see Agent Contacts for phone and office hours.
2. **Or call the 24/7 claims hotline:** **1-800-555-CLAIM (2524)**
3. **Seek medical attention** if you feel any pain, even if minor. Some injuries (whiplash, soft tissue) don't present immediately.
4. **File a police report** if an officer did not come to the scene. Go to the local police station or file online.

## Step-by-Step Claims Filing Process

### Step 1: Report the Claim

**Who to contact:**
- **Your agent:** Sarah Chen — (555) 234-5678 (Mon-Fri 8am-6pm)
- **24/7 Claims Line:** 1-800-555-CLAIM (2524)
- **Online portal:** claims.greatlakesmutual.example.com

**Information you'll need:**
- Policy number: PLX-2026-4481
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Description of what happened
- Other driver's information (name, insurance, plate number)
- Police report number
- Photos of the damage

### Step 2: Claim Assignment

Within **1 business day**, a claims adjuster will be assigned to your case. They will:
- Contact you to review the incident
- Explain your coverage and deductible
- Schedule a vehicle inspection

### Step 3: Vehicle Inspection

Options for inspection:
- **Preferred body shop:** The adjuster will recommend nearby shops in your area
- **Independent appraiser:** If you prefer, an appraiser can come to your location
- **Upload photos:** For minor damage, you may be asked to submit photos through the portal

The adjuster will prepare a damage estimate within **3-5 business days** of inspection.

### Step 4: Repair Authorization

Once the estimate is approved:
- You choose the repair shop (your choice, not the insurer's)
- The insurer pays the shop directly, minus your **$500 deductible** (collision) or **$250 deductible** (comprehensive)
- If repairs exceed the estimate, the shop contacts the adjuster for a supplement

### Step 5: Rental Car

If your vehicle needs repairs:
- **Rental reimbursement:** Up to $40/day for up to 30 days
- Call Enterprise, Hertz, or National and provide your claim number
- Or your agent can arrange direct billing

### Step 6: Claim Resolution

- **Liability determination:** Usually within 2-4 weeks
- **Subrogation:** If the other driver was at fault, your insurer will pursue their insurer to recover your deductible
- **Total loss:** If repair costs exceed 75% of vehicle value, the vehicle may be declared a total loss. You'd receive the actual cash value of the vehicle minus your deductible.

## Uninsured Motorist (UM) Claim Procedure

If the other driver has no insurance:

1. **File a police report within 24 hours** (mandatory for UM claims)
2. Report the claim as described above, specifying "uninsured motorist"
3. Your UM coverage applies: up to $100,000/$300,000 bodily injury, $25,000 property damage
4. **No deductible** applies to UM bodily injury claims
5. **$250 deductible** applies to UM property damage claims
6. The claims timeline is typically **longer** for UM claims (4-8 weeks) due to additional investigation
7. Your insurer may pursue the uninsured driver directly for recovery

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't delay reporting** — even if damage seems minor
- **Don't admit fault** to the other driver or their insurer
- **Don't sign anything** from the other driver's insurance company without consulting your agent
- **Don't skip the police report** — especially for UM claims
- **Don't post about the accident on social media** — it can be used against your claim

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will filing a claim raise my rates?**  
A: Not-at-fault claims generally do not affect your premium. At-fault claims may result in a surcharge at renewal.

**Q: What if the other driver's insurance contacts me?**  
A: You're not obligated to speak with them. Refer them to your agent (Sarah Chen) or your claims adjuster.

**Q: Can I choose my own repair shop?**  
A: Yes. You have the right to choose any licensed repair shop. Using a preferred shop may speed up the estimate process.

**Q: What if I discover additional damage after repairs begin?**  
A: The repair shop will file a supplement request with your adjuster. This is common and covered under your claim.
